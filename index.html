TikTok Boredom and Attention

I set out to answer the question if TikTok actually rots your brain more than other content. I did this through having some people watch TikTok or longer videos and see if their state boredom would increase, and attention would decrease afterward. I mainly found that it did not take until the 5th day for only attention scores to differ between experiemnt and control. Prior use was controlled but similar to previous research did relate to higher boredom and lower attention span. TikTok does not seem to cause more boredom or attention issues in short consumption. However, overttime it could have long lasting effects. If I were to do more research, I would look at TikTok use over a longer period of time and study binge watching instead of shorter uses. CIMPT

I helped my teachor and advisor, professor Andy Hertel as well as 5 other students test an intervention to help those who smoke to stop smoking. This intervention is called the CIMP-T. The CIMP-T stands for cannabis-user identity misfit perception task. Based off the prior created and tested DIMP-T which was used for drinkers. It is a task for cannabis users to write the bad qualities of a someone who uses cannabis, understand that they do not have those qualities, and then lessen their smoking because they see themselves as different. Grandparents And Giving

Me and a group set out to figure out the question of which grandparents provides the most for their grandchild and if there was a cultural difference between individualistic or collectivistic. We sent out a school wide survey asking to see which of the four biological grandparents (mother's mother, mother's father, father's mother, and father's father) invest the most in their grandchild in the realms of emotional closeness, percieved liking, communication, and financial support. We found that the grandparents did not differ accross culture, but the mother's grandparents invested significantly more tp their grandchildren in all categories except financial support. Marijuana and Attention

As seen above, I created two cognitive games for making an app. I have conducted a psychological test on one of these apps (add the last 2). Here I tested if marijuana actually affected working memory. I had a within subjects design where participants played the game sober and when they were inebriated. We found that participants who played the game inebriated performed significantly worse on the attention game than when they played the game sober. It was near significant that the difference between a participant's inebriated and sober socre was smaller when the participant was a frequent user of marijuana.
